## The Three Storylines That Actually Matter

### 1. Pool D Is Effectively a Knockout Round

Dominican Republic and Venezuela are both legitimate championship contenders. They meet in Pool D, meaning one of them exits before the quarterfinals. This is the single biggest structural distortion in the 2026 WBC. The winner arrives in Miami with battle experience; the loser goes home with legitimate gripes about bracket luck.

**Our lean:** DR (-160 vs Venezuela in pool play per early lines) advances from Pool D. Venezuela ‚Äî the 2023 team that went 4-0 and still lost to a Turner grand slam ‚Äî becomes the most devastating early exit in WBC history.

### 2. Can Japan Win Without Their 2023 Pitching Staff?

Roki Sasaki, Yu Darvish, Shota Imanaga: all absent. Three of the five rotation starters who won the championship in 2023 aren't here. Yamamoto as the clear ace is enough to beat most teams. But to beat the USA ‚Äî which has Skenes and Skubal ‚Äî the depth behind Yamamoto needs to hold.

**Our lean:** Japan reaches the final. Whether they win it depends on which version of their bullpen shows up.

### 3. Skubal's One Start Changes the USA's Tournament Math

Tarik Skubal ‚Äî arguably the best pitcher in baseball right now ‚Äî is being held to a single start due to load management. In a tournament with 4-5 games in two weeks, that's a significant concession. It effectively makes Paul Skenes a one-man pitching advantage for Team USA in the knockout rounds.

The 2023 final ‚Äî Ohtani striking out Mike Trout to end it ‚Äî is still the greatest moment in WBC history. A rematch is the storyline everyone wants. Here's how they compare:
